🏗️ About the ISS

The International Space Station is the largest modular space station in low Earth orbit. A joint project of NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, JAXA, and CSA, it has been continuously occupied since November 2000.

📡 Tracking Data

ISS position is updated every 5 seconds using real-time orbital data. The station orbits Earth approximately 16 times per day at an inclination of 51.6°, allowing it to pass over 90% of the inhabited world.

🎥 Live Feed

The HD Earth Viewing Experiment streams live video from cameras mounted on the ISS exterior. During orbital night or signal loss, the screen may appear dark — the station is simply passing over the night side of Earth.
